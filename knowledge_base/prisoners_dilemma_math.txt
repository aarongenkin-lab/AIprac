III. Survey Results
a. Classic Prisonerâ€™s Dilemma

226 participants were surveyed.
Results:

54% chose to testify (defect)

46% remained silent (join)

Motivations included trust, guilt, conscience, or minimizing sentence length.

b. Climate Change Policy

Participants were asked whether to sign a climate treaty resembling the Kyoto Protocol.

Results:

36.3% chose not to sign

Nearly two-thirds chose to sign despite the Nash Equilibrium favoring defection

c. Drugs in Baseball

A non-equilibrium scenario involving performance-enhancing drugs.

Results:

84.1% chose not to use drugs

Decisions were largely driven by health concerns rather than rules or ethics

V. Conclusion

While mathematics favors defection, real people frequently choose cooperation. Trust, empathy, and moral reasoning often override pure optimization. Ironically, defectors consistently received disproportionate rewards.

Across all scenarios:

33.2% joined in all three cases

Only 6.6% defected in all three

Mathematically, defection dominatesâ€”but human behavior resists this conclusion.
